About France

How is France ?

Geography

France is about 80% the size of Texas. In the Alps near the Italian and Swiss borders is western Europe's highest point—Mont Blanc. The forest-covered Vosges Mountains are in the northeast, and the Pyrénées are along the Spanish border. Except for extreme northern France, the country may be described as four river basins and a plateau.

Culture

France has been a centre of Western cultural development for centuries. Many French artists have been among the most renowned of their time, and France is still recognised in the world for its rich cultural tradition.

Church

Religion

France is a secular country, and freedom of religion is a constitutional right. French religious policy is based on the concept of laïcité, a strict separation of church and state under which public life is kept completely secular.
Catholicism has been the predominant religion in France for more than a millennium, though it is not as actively practised today as it was. Among the 47,000 religious buildings in France, 94% are Roman Catholic.

Food

French cuisine consists of the cooking traditions and practices from France. There are many dishes that are considered part of French national cuisine today. A meal often consists of three courses, introductory course, main course, dessert, sometimes with a salad offered before the dessert. Typical introductory course include, for example, terrine, foie Gras, main course include white fish, steak, dessert include cream brulee, crepe. In addition to the French national cuisine, there are also regional cuisine. They are popular not only in fine dining restaurants but also in general homes.
